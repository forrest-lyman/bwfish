# Barracuda

Barracuda are edge holders. They like clean water, bait lanes, kelp edges, reef lines, channels, and current breaks where they can watch before committing. They may strike violently, but much of their fishing is about inspection.

The fish can be visible and not ready. They can follow three times, turn away, and finally eat when the angle changes.

## Edges And Visibility

Barracuda use water clarity. They often hold where they can see bait crossing a line: reef edge, grass edge, kelp stringer, channel, dock shadow, or color change.

Useful signs:

- bait nervous along the edge
- fish suspended outside structure
- current pulling bait past ambush water
- follows from the same lane
- strikes after a lure changes speed
- birds or small predators working nearby

The edge gives the fish a station. Current gives the bait movement.

## Follows And Commitment

Barracuda often show themselves before eating. A follow is useful if the boat reads what changed.

They commit better when:

- the lure accelerates away
- bait crosses current naturally
- the cast lands beyond the fish
- the retrieve stays in the lane
- light or chop reduces caution

They refuse when:

- the lure comes straight at them
- the water is too clear and still
- pressure has already educated the group
- bait is present but not being moved

## Regional Shape

Southern California barracuda often move with anchovy, sardine, squid, kelp, and coastal current. Tropical barracuda use reefs, flats edges, channels, wrecks, and bait schools. In both places, they are less random than they look.

Barracuda are not just toothy fish in clear water. They are readers of lanes.
